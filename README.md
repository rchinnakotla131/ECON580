# ECON580

## Overview
This paper examines the relationship between social and demographic determinants from communities in Gujarat and the intensity of inter-communal riots after an exogenous shock. In February 2002, a train bombing in Godhra, Gujarat, caused large riots lasting months throughout the state, with scholarship finding media, migration, and religious composition as potential factors for violence. Using demographic data from the 1991 and 2001 Indian census, along with data on the destruction of Muslim religious buildings, I construct a model that exploits demographic variation across districts to determine how social factors can determine riot intensity. Findings show that there are no statistically significant effects that these factors of interest, language distribution, and religious migration, have on riot intensity. However, the signs of all independent variables follow previous literature. These results suggest that there should be more empirical work done to understand the causes of the intensity of riots in Gujarat.

## Data
- **Source:** 2001 Indian Census, 1991 Indian Census, CJP Report on Muslim Monuments Destroyed
- **Variables:** Key variables used in the analysis
